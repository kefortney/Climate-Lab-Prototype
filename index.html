Climate Kitchen: Foundations for Planetary‑Health Cooking

This open, non‑credit site translates the Climate Kitchen’s research‑informed approach into practical, at‑home learning. You’ll practice core kitchen competencies while aligning everyday cooking with sustainability through our five tenets.

What you’ll practice

  • Build food agency and confidence (Empower & Enact)
  • Organize your space and plan before you cook (Mise En Place)
  • Develop your palate and vocabulary (Sensory Analysis)
  • Sequence tasks and plan meals across time (Sequence & Planning)
  • Use knives safely and effectively (Knife Skills)
  • Control heat for delicious, safe results (Time & Temperature)

Our pedagogy emphasizes inquiry‑based, hands‑on learning and integrates the Climate Kitchen’s sustainability tenets throughout.

Five Tenets of Sustainability

  • Plant forward
  • Integrating tastes & habits
  • Low waste
  • Whole food utilization
  • Local & regional sourcing

Learn more on UVM’s Tenets of Sustainability.

Why Climate Kitchen?

The Climate Kitchen is a makerspace for collaboration and experimentation that reimagines the purpose of a kitchen—linking food preparation, sensory learning, and sustainability to support individual and planetary health.
