Sensory Analysis

Goal: Build a shared language for taste, aroma, and texture so you can season with intention and design meals people love.

Learn & Try

  • Review basic tastes (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami) and common descriptors.
  • Keep a sensory journal—capture observations with each cook.
  • Use a “Flavor Star” to balance tastes and aromas.
  • Run quick rank or preference tests with friends/family.

Hedonic Tests

The hedonic scale is a sensory evaluation tool used to measure the degree of pleasure or liking of a product or service. The scale usually consists of 9 levels ranging from 1 to 9, or "dislike extremely" to "like extremely". The hedonic scale is widely used for consumer acceptance testing.

The Flavor Star

Use this framework to understand how different tastes interact:

  • Enhances: Brings out the other flavor
  • Balances: Counteracts the dominant flavor, adding dimension to dish if experiencing too much of one flavor
  • Clashes: When two tastes are clashing, adding another balancing flavor to level it out

The Flavor Star diagram shows the five basic tastes (Salty/Umami, Spice, Sweet, Sour, Bitter) and their relationships. Each taste can enhance, balance, or clash with others to create harmonious or discordant flavor profiles.
